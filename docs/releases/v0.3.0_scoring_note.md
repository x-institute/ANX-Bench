# ANX-Bench v0.3.0 Candidate Scoring Note

## Release status

Version `v0.3.0` is a preregistered provisional scoring candidate package. It is not a citable scored ANX-Bench release, does not authorize official item-level or aggregate scoring, and must not be used as evidence that the somatic and ambient candidate construct has passed psychometric validation.

The v0.3.0 manifest records `official_scored_items: []` and `scoring_eligibility.aggregate_scoring_permitted: false`. The four somatic and ambient items remain `development_only` with `validation.scoring_eligible: false` until a later release records non-future-dated observed fielding evidence, passes the validation dossier gate, and passes the release validator.

The next required artifact before any scored ANX-Bench claim from this candidate is `docs/releases/v0.3_to_v0.3.1_promotion_gate.md`. Maintainers must satisfy that gate before creating a citable `v0.3.1` release, including validation of `validation/v0.2/somatic_ambient_anxiety/observed_wave1_results.json`, replacement of all placeholder zero hashes, retained-item approval, and reviewer signoff.

## Candidate construct

The candidate construct is `somatic_ambient_anxiety`, a four-item somatic and ambient anxiety construct in the `somatic_ambient` domain. It is intended to measure AI-linked somatic and ambient anxiety expressed as sleep disruption, bodily vigilance, background dread, and avoidance after standardized exposure to AI capability news, model releases, cumulative AI progress, or a credible AI capability demonstration.

If post-fielding validation passes in a later release, the proposed construct score would be the mean of retained observed item scores after respondent-level exclusions and item missingness rules are applied. Each retained item would be scored from 1 to 5 using its frozen v0.2.0 scoring key. Higher scores would indicate stronger self-reported somatic and ambient anxiety in response to the standardized AI scenarios. The score would not be a clinical diagnosis, medical screening result, or measure of generalized anxiety disorder, panic, insomnia, or health anxiety.

## Conditional interpretation

If a later release approves the construct after post-fielding validation, these descriptive bands are the preregistered candidate interpretation for the four-item mean:

| Mean score | Candidate interpretation |
| ---: | --- |
| 1.00 to 2.00 | Low somatic and ambient AI anxiety under the standardized scenarios |
| 2.01 to 3.50 | Moderate somatic and ambient AI anxiety, with noticeable but not severe response elevation |
| 3.51 to 5.00 | High somatic and ambient AI anxiety, with strong arousal, dread, sleep disruption, or avoidance endorsement |

These bands are not active benchmark score bands in v0.3.0. They do not define clinical severity, treatment need, workplace fitness, vulnerability status, or individual risk classification.

## Conditional comparisons

Only a later scored release may permit comparisons. If post-fielding validation passes without item wording, anchor, scoring-key, language, mode, or timing changes, the preregistered candidate comparison scope is:

- Cross-sectional US English adult comparisons of the `somatic_ambient_anxiety` construct mean.
- `somatic_ambient` domain summaries based only on retained and approved somatic and ambient items.
- Repeated-wave comparisons within the same US English online administration design.
- Event-study use as an outcome when the event registry, exposure window, baseline window, and respondent inclusion rules are preregistered before outcome inspection.
- Subgroup comparisons only where confirmation-sample cell sizes, DIF, and invariance evidence support them.

## Disallowed uses in v0.3.0

`v0.3.0` does not permit:

- Official item-level, construct, domain, overall, longitudinal, or event-study scoring.
- Citation as a scored ANX-Bench benchmark release.
- Claims that somatic and ambient anxiety items were fielded, retained, or psychometrically approved.
- Clinical screening, diagnosis, individual treatment triage, or claims about anxiety disorders.
- Translation-based comparisons or cross-country comparisons without a bridge dossier.
- Comparisons using altered item wording, altered anchors, non-online administration, different scenario timing, or revised scoring keys.
- IRT theta scoring, item-parameter linking, or cross-version equating.
- Overall ANX-Bench headline scoring across all domains.

## Required promotion evidence

A later release may promote the candidate only after the post-fielding dossier records observed, checksum-bound evidence for EFA, CFA, reliability, graded-response IRT, DIF, invariance, external validity, item retention, exclusion rates, analytic sample sizes, and independent reviewer signoff. The exact pass/fail checklist is `docs/releases/v0.3_to_v0.3.1_promotion_gate.md`. The release manifest must list any approved items in `official_scored_items`, set scoring eligibility consistently in the item files and dossier, checksum `validation/v0.2/somatic_ambient_anxiety/observed_wave1_results.json` with a nonzero digest, and pass the future-date guard in `tools/validate_release.py`.
