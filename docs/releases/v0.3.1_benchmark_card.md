# ANX-Bench v0.3.1 Benchmark Card

## Citation Status

`ANX-Bench v0.3.1` is the current citable scored release for the somatic and ambient AI anxiety construct. The canonical machine-readable release definition is `releases/v0.3.1/manifest.json`; this card is the human-readable companion for citation, scope control, and reviewer audit.

The release authorizes one construct score only. It does not authorize an overall ANX-Bench score, cross-domain score, clinical interpretation, translated administration, non-online administration, IRT theta score, or use outside the preregistered US English online Wave 1 style administration design.

## Scored Construct

The scored construct is `somatic_ambient_anxiety`, labeled "Somatic and ambient AI anxiety" in the validation dossier. It measures AI-linked somatic and ambient anxiety expressed as sleep disruption, bodily vigilance, background dread, and avoidance after exposure to standardized AI capability news, model releases, cumulative AI progress, or credible AI capability demonstrations.

Higher scores indicate stronger self-reported somatic and ambient anxiety under the released scenarios. The score is not a diagnosis, treatment screen, measure of generalized anxiety disorder, panic, insomnia, health anxiety, workplace fitness, or individual vulnerability classification.

## Official Item IDs

The official scored item set contains exactly these four retained `v0.2.0` item versions:

| Item ID | Content facet | Release status |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `sleep_disruption_ai_news` | Difficulty settling and sleep disruption after late-evening AI capability news | `approved_scored` |
| `body_vigilance_model_release` | Pulse, breathing, muscle tension, and bodily alertness after a major model release | `approved_scored` |
| `background_dread_ai_progress` | Diffuse background dread after cumulative AI progress across social domains | `approved_scored` |
| `avoidance_after_ai_capability_demo` | Anxiety-linked avoidance of additional AI information after a credible capability demonstration | `approved_scored` |

No other ANX-Bench item, anchoring vignette, behavioral criterion task, covariate, or comparator variable is part of the v0.3.1 official scored item set.

## Population Scope

The validated population scope is United States adults aged 18 or older eligible for the `anx_us_2026w02_somatic` calibration survey, recruited through quota-balanced professional online panels. The fielded validation evidence used a development pilot analytic sample of `N = 520` and an independent confirmation analytic sample of `N = 1109` after preregistered exclusions. The observed validation artifact reports `N = 1629` analytic respondents across both samples.

Claims for other countries, languages, age ranges outside the adult scope, clinical populations, organizational populations, high-risk subgroups, or altered recruitment frames require a new bridge or validation dossier before citation as ANX-Bench benchmark evidence.

## Administration Mode

The authorized administration mode is online, English-language, US adult survey administration using the frozen `anx_us_2026w02_somatic` instrument materials, frozen item wording, frozen response anchors, frozen item versions, preregistered quality-control rules, and the `no_event` event status for the calibration wave. Item order may follow the frozen instrument and preregistration rules only.

The non-scored response-scale anchoring vignettes and the non-scored revealed AI review allocation behavioral task remain validation and calibration artifacts. They must not be converted into ANX-Bench respondent-item rows or included in the construct score.

## Score Formula

The v0.3.1 score is `somatic_ambient_anxiety_mean`.

For each eligible respondent after preregistered respondent-level exclusions:

```text
somatic_ambient_anxiety_mean =
  mean(scored_value_sleep_disruption_ai_news,
       scored_value_body_vigilance_model_release,
       scored_value_background_dread_ai_progress,
       scored_value_avoidance_after_ai_capability_demo)
```

Each official item is scored from 1 to 5 using its frozen v0.2.0 scoring key. Higher values indicate stronger endorsement of the released somatic and ambient anxiety response. Skipped item responses remain missing and must not be imputed. Reports must state item-level observed counts, missingness, exclusions, and whether the respondent or wave estimate used all four observed item scores or a preregistered missingness rule from the frozen wave packet.

The release permits only this construct-level mean for `somatic_ambient_anxiety`. It does not permit IRT theta scoring, item-parameter linking, cross-version equating, cross-domain aggregation, or an overall ANX-Bench headline score.

## Allowed Claims

Users may make the following claims when analyses use the released item files, release manifest, construct registry, scoring rules, and administration scope:

- `ANX-Bench v0.3.1` provides a citable scored construct measure of `somatic_ambient_anxiety` for US English online adult survey administration.
- The four official item IDs listed in this card are approved scored items for the v0.3.1 somatic and ambient construct score.
- The construct score is the respondent-level arithmetic mean of the released 1 to 5 item scores after preregistered exclusions and missingness handling.
- The observed Wave 1 validation evidence passed the preregistered CFA, omega, IRT, DIF, invariance, external-validity, retention, and release-gate checks for the restricted v0.3.1 construct scope.
- Cross-sectional reports of somatic and ambient AI anxiety are permitted within the validated US English online adult administration design, subject to the release manifest, preregistration, and wave packet.
- Event-study or repeated-wave use may treat `somatic_ambient_anxiety_mean` as an outcome only when a later wave or event design is preregistered before outcome inspection and does not alter wording, anchors, language, mode, timing, or scoring without a bridge dossier.

## Blocked Claims

Users must not claim any of the following from v0.3.1:

- An overall ANX-Bench score, cross-domain score, or headline population anxiety index.
- Clinical diagnosis, screening, treatment need, medical triage, workplace fitness, vulnerability status, or individual risk classification.
- Comparability across countries, languages, translations, non-online modes, revised wording, revised response anchors, changed scenario timing, or altered exclusion rules.
- IRT theta scoring, item-parameter linking, cross-version equating, or latent-score comparability beyond the released mean score.
- Validation of anchoring vignettes or the revealed AI review allocation task as ANX-Bench scored items.
- Subgroup score claims for sparse cells or groups without adequate DIF and invariance evidence.
- Longitudinal or event-study causal claims unless the relevant wave, event registry, baseline window, exposure window, inclusion rules, and outcome model were preregistered before outcome inspection.
