# ANX-Bench v0.3.2 Retest Benchmark Card

## Citation Status

`ANX-Bench v0.3.2` is a citable evidence release for 14-day repeatability of the existing `v0.3.1` somatic and ambient anxiety construct score. The canonical machine-readable release definition is `releases/v0.3.2/manifest.json`. The observed retest evidence packet is `validation/v0.3/somatic_ambient_retest/wave3_retest_evidence.json`.

This release does not create a new scored construct definition. The source scored construct definition remains `ANX-Bench v0.3.1`, including the same four `v0.2.0` item files, same scoring rule, same US English online adult administration scope, and same restriction against overall ANX, cross-domain, clinical, translated, non-online, or IRT theta claims.

## Authorized Repeatability Claim

Reports may cite `v0.3.2` for exactly this claim:

```text
Within the v0.3.1 US English online adult administration scope, the released four-item somatic_ambient_anxiety_mean has checksum-bound 14-day test-retest evidence. The observed Wave 3 no-event retest packet passed the preregistered ICC(2,1), mean-change, item-stability, attrition, longitudinal-invariance, and panel-conditioning gates for short-interval repeatability.
```

The authorized claim is repeatability evidence for the already approved `somatic_ambient_anxiety_mean`. It is not evidence that population anxiety changed, that an AI event caused a response, that the score is clinically meaningful, or that the score generalizes outside the original administration scope.

## Evidence Summary

The table below summarizes the checksum-bound observed evidence in `validation/v0.3/somatic_ambient_retest/wave3_retest_evidence.json`.

| Field | v0.3.2 observed retest evidence |
| --- | --- |
| Linkage and analytic counts | `780` Wave 1 respondents were eligible for recontact and invited; `19` were bounced or unreachable; `651` started retest; `642` consented; `621` completed before exclusions; `613` unique linked pairs were identified; `563` complete pairs fell in the primary 13 to 16 day window; `541` pairs remained in the final primary analytic sample. |
| Attrition | Compared covariates included Wave 1 score, all four items, demographics, AI exposure, baseline general anxiety, device type, and response time. The maximum absolute standardized mean difference was `0.083`; maximum weighted percentage-point difference was `4.4`; Wave 1 construct mean difference was `0.07` points or `0.11` SD. Attrition was not classified as sensitive after the preregistered weighting adjustment. Decision: `pass`. |
| ICC(2,1) | Two-way random-effects absolute-agreement single-measure ICC(2,1) was `0.742`, 95 percent CI `[0.651, 0.812]`, with `2000` bootstrap resamples and weighted sensitivity estimate `0.735`. Decision: `pass`. |
| Mean change | Weighted Wave 1 mean was `2.71`; weighted retest mean was `2.67`; weighted mean change was `-0.04`, 95 percent CI `[-0.101, 0.021]`; standardized response mean was `-0.055`. Decision: `pass`. |
| Item stability | All four items passed. Unweighted stability correlations ranged from `0.55` to `0.61`; weighted exact agreement ranged from `0.46` to `0.50`; weighted adjacent agreement ranged from `0.86` to `0.90`; two-or-more category movement ranged from `0.09` to `0.13`. |
| Longitudinal invariance | The ordinal one-factor retest model converged and preserved the one-factor structure. Metric delta CFI was `-0.005`; metric delta RMSEA was `0.006`; scalar delta CFI was `-0.008`; scalar delta RMSEA was `0.009`; maximum expected construct-score impact was `0.058`; maximum same-item residual correlation was `0.136`. Decision: `pass`. |
| Panel conditioning | Excluding `43` conditioning-sensitive cases yielded ICC `0.724` and weighted mean change `-0.018`; absolute mean-change difference from the primary estimate was `0.022`. Panel conditioning sensitivity was not flagged. Decision: `pass`. |
| Final decision | Construct repeatability decision: `pass`. Item-level failed item count: `0`. Overall retest evidence decision: `pass`. Claim authorized: `true` for the narrow 14-day repeatability statement only. |

## Required Citation Target

The required citation target for repeatability claims is:

```text
ANX-Bench v0.3.2. Release manifest: releases/v0.3.2/manifest.json.
Retest benchmark card: docs/releases/v0.3.2_retest_benchmark_card.md.
Observed retest evidence: validation/v0.3/somatic_ambient_retest/wave3_retest_evidence.json.
Source scored construct definition: ANX-Bench v0.3.1, releases/v0.3.1/manifest.json.
```

Any paper, report, dataset, or derived benchmark claim must identify both `v0.3.2` as the repeatability evidence release and `v0.3.1` as the source scored construct definition.

## Blocked Claims

The following claims are explicitly blocked by `v0.3.2`:

- Trend claims: `v0.3.2` does not authorize statements that somatic and ambient AI anxiety increased, decreased, remained stable in the population, or followed a time trend.
- Event-study claims: `v0.3.2` does not authorize event-window, pre-post event, treated-control, interrupted time-series, or event-study estimates.
- Causal-shock claims: `v0.3.2` does not authorize claims that any AI model release, capability demonstration, policy change, media cycle, or external event caused a psychological response.
- Overall ANX claims: `v0.3.2` does not authorize an overall ANX-Bench score, headline AI anxiety index, or benchmark-wide aggregate.
- Cross-domain claims: `v0.3.2` does not authorize economic-vocational, epistemic, autonomy-surveillance, relational, safety-catastrophic, existential-identity, or cross-domain scoring.
- Clinical claims: `v0.3.2` does not authorize diagnosis, screening, treatment triage, medical severity classification, workplace fitness assessment, individual vulnerability labeling, or clinical monitoring.
- Generalization claims: `v0.3.2` does not authorize claims for non-US populations, non-English administrations, translations, non-online modes, altered item wording, altered response anchors, new panels without bridge evidence, or retest intervals other than the preregistered 14-day window.
