# ANX-Bench v0.4.0 to v0.4.1 Event-Study Evidence Promotion Gate

This document is the release-blocking gate for converting the frozen `v0.4.0` Wave 4 somatic event-study packet into a citable `v0.4.1` event-study evidence release. It applies only to the source-scored `somatic_ambient_anxiety_mean` inherited from `v0.3.1` and only to the preregistered Wave 4 event-study design.

Passing this gate authorizes one narrow claim: `event-associated somatic anxiety change`. It does not authorize causal language, overall ANX-Bench scoring, clinical anxiety interpretation, cross-domain scoring, national prevalence, long-term trend claims, subgroup headline claims, or policy-decision language.

The authoritative threshold source is `analysis/v0.4/somatic_event_study/wave4_event_study_analysis_plan.json`. If this promotion gate and the analysis plan disagree about sample thresholds, event windows, coefficient thresholds, p-values, confidence intervals, pretrend checks, robustness checks, or confound rules, the analysis plan controls.

## Required Observed Artifacts

Promotion to `v0.4.1` requires all artifacts below to exist and agree before release labeling:

```text
validation/v0.4/somatic_event_study/wave4_event_study_evidence.json
events/v0.4/anx_us_2026w04_somatic_event_registry.json
analysis/v0.4/somatic_event_study/wave4_event_study_analysis_plan.json
docs/preregistrations/anx_us_2026w04_somatic_event_study.md
docs/instruments/anx_us_2026w04_somatic_event_instrument.md
releases/v0.4.1/manifest.json
```

The evidence packet must validate with:

```bash
python3 tools/validate_event_study_evidence.py validation/v0.4/somatic_event_study/wave4_event_study_evidence.json
python3 tools/validate_release.py releases/v0.4.1/manifest.json
```

The `v0.4.1` manifest must checksum the observed event-study evidence packet, locked event registry, preregistration, instrument, analysis plan, source `v0.3.1` scoring artifacts, and any archived analysis script, session lock, restricted-input hash manifest, coder signoff, and reviewer signoff used to produce the observed evidence. No required checksum may be zero-filled.

## Machine-Checkable Evidence Gate

The observed evidence packet must use `schema/event_study_evidence.schema.json` and must record all fields required by `tools/validate_event_study_evidence.py`:

- locked non-`no_event` event ID
- registry version, lock date, lock status, pre-outcome lock proof, outcome-inspection status at lock, registry SHA-256, and event-record SHA-256
- baseline, follow-up, excluded event-buffer, and placebo-pretrend windows matching the locked analysis plan
- nominal and effective weighted N for baseline and follow-up
- primary `beta_post_event` coefficient, standard error, 95 percent confidence interval, p-value, and pass-fail decision
- placebo-pretrend coefficient, confidence interval, p-value, and pass-fail decision
- all robustness checks listed in the analysis plan, in the locked order
- required robustness coefficients for the unweighted primary model, alternative weight trim, and narrow follow-up window
- competing-event and release-blocking confound review
- final claim decision and exact permitted claim language

The evidence packet may remain a null-valued template before fielding, but a completed packet cannot authorize a claim unless every locked threshold passes.

## Promotion Criteria

The release may be promoted only if all criteria below are true:

1. The event registry is locked before outcome inspection and records a qualifying non-`no_event` event.
2. The registry proof records `registry_status: locked`, nonzero SHA-256 digests, and no release-blocking confound.
3. Baseline and follow-up nominal N each meet the analysis-plan minimum of `750`.
4. Baseline and follow-up effective weighted N each meet the analysis-plan minimum of `500`.
5. The primary coefficient is positive and at least `0.15` points.
6. The primary p-value is no greater than `0.05`.
7. The primary 95 percent confidence-interval lower bound is at least `0.03`.
8. The placebo pretrend absolute coefficient is no greater than `0.10` and is not statistically significant.
9. The unweighted primary, alternative weight-trim, and narrow-window robustness coefficients are each at least `0.10`.
10. The confound review finds no release-blocking competing event or design violation.
11. The evidence packet records `claim_authorized: true` only when all preceding checks pass.
12. The reported claim text uses the exact permitted language and contains no causal, clinical, overall ANX, long-term, or cross-domain claim.

## Blocked Outcomes

Any condition below blocks promotion and limits the release to descriptive monitoring:

- missing or schema-invalid observed evidence
- event registry not locked before outcome inspection
- locked event ID still set to `no_event`
- zero-filled, missing, or malformed registry or event-record checksum
- baseline or follow-up nominal N below `750`
- baseline or follow-up effective weighted N below `500`
- primary coefficient below `0.15`, non-positive, p-value above `0.05`, or lower 95 percent confidence bound below `0.03`
- significant or substantively large placebo pretrend
- required robustness coefficient below `0.10`
- release-blocking confound or unresolved competing event
- claim decision inconsistent with the machine-computed threshold decision
- reported language that states or implies causality, clinical anxiety, overall ANX, cross-domain movement, national prevalence, or long-term change

When blocked, maintainers may archive the observed evidence as a non-citable descriptive record. They must not publish `v0.4.1` as supporting event-study evidence, must not mark event-study claims as authorized, and must not alter the source `v0.3.1` item set to rescue the event-study result.

## Permitted Claim Language

If the gate passes, ANX-Bench materials may say:

```text
Wave 4 provides evidence of event-associated somatic anxiety change in the preregistered US English online adult sample and follow-up window.
```

Equivalent wording is allowed only if it preserves all of these limits: event-associated, somatic anxiety, preregistered sample, and preregistered follow-up window.

ANX-Bench materials must not say:

- the AI event caused anxiety
- the release changed overall ANX
- ANX-Bench detected a national anxiety shift
- the finding is clinical anxiety evidence
- the finding generalizes beyond the sampled US English online adult frame
- the finding persists beyond the preregistered follow-up window
- subgroup, item-level, cross-domain, or policy claims are established by the Wave 4 event-study result
