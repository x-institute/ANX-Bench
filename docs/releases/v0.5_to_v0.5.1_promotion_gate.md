# ANX-Bench v0.5.0 to v0.5.1 Economic-Vocational Scored-Release Promotion Gate

This document is the release-blocking checklist for converting the frozen `v0.5.0` economic-vocational calibration packet into a citable scored `v0.5.1` release. It applies only to the `economic_vocational_anxiety` construct and only to the four frozen `v0.1.0` item versions listed in `releases/v0.5.0/manifest.json`:

- `skill_obsolescence_software`
- `wage_pressure_customer_support`
- `retraining_pressure_accounting`
- `status_loss_creative_work`

The authoritative threshold source is `analysis/v0.5/economic_vocational/wave5_analysis_plan.json`. If this promotion gate and the analysis plan disagree, the analysis plan controls for psychometric thresholds, sample definitions, exclusion rules, and preregistered model specifications. This gate controls only the release decision for `v0.5.1`.

Passing this gate authorizes only the retained economic-vocational construct mean named `economic_vocational_anxiety_mean`. It does not authorize overall ANX-Bench scoring, cross-domain scoring, longitudinal claims, event-study claims, labor-market trend claims, causal claims about AI capabilities, clinical interpretation, translation claims, or use outside the preregistered US English online administration design.

Failing any required check keeps the release non-citable for scored economic-vocational ANX-Bench claims.

## Pass Checklist

The release may be promoted only if every item below is true.

1. **Artifact integrity**
   - `observed_wave5_results.json` exists at the required path and validates with `tools/validate_observed_validation_results.py`.
   - `wave5_calibration_dossier.json` exists at the required path and validates with `tools/validate_validation_dossier.py`.
   - `wave5_evidence_provenance.json` and `wave5_evidence_manifest.json` exist, are internally consistent, and identify the exact raw inputs, scripts or notebooks, software session, generated outputs, and reviewer records.
   - `releases/v0.5.1/manifest.json` validates with `tools/validate_release.py`.
   - No required artifact contains a zero-filled checksum placeholder.
   - The manifest, evidence provenance, evidence manifest, validation dossier, observed results, and item files agree on the retained item IDs and scoring eligibility decisions.

2. **Sample, split, and exclusion integrity**
   - The development pilot uses the preregistered target `N=500` role and the confirmation sample uses the preregistered target `N=1000` role, subject only to documented eligibility and exclusion flow.
   - The development pilot and confirmation sample are non-overlapping by respondent, panel identifier, household linkage, and protected linkage token.
   - Primary pass-fail decisions use only analytically eligible respondents after the preregistered adult US English consent, nonoverlap, attention, comprehension, economic-vocational attribution, speeding, straightline plus quality failure, excessive ANX missingness, duplicate or unusable record, and self-reported nonunderstanding exclusions.
   - Unemployment, low income, career transition, high perceived occupational AI exposure, and high baseline general anxiety are retained as DIF, validity, or sensitivity variables and are not used as exclusions by themselves.
   - Weighted descriptive summaries are reported, but unweighted primary EFA, CFA, omega, IRT, DIF, invariance, and external-validity decisions are used unless the analysis plan permits a validated survey-weight implementation.

3. **Item retention and distribution gate**
   - At least `3` of the four frozen economic-vocational items are retained for construct scoring.
   - Every retained item has confirmation-sample item missingness no greater than `0.10`.
   - Every retained item has public subgroup item missingness no greater than `0.15` for any subgroup comparison the release proposes to describe.
   - No retained item has more than `0.70` of valid responses in one response category.
   - No retained item has more than `0.85` of valid responses in two adjacent response categories unless the dossier gives a preregistered, reviewer-approved extreme-targeting justification supported by adequate IRT information.
   - Every retained item preserves the frozen respondent-facing wording, response anchors, scoring key, scenario text, construct metadata, and `item_version: v0.1.0`.

4. **EFA gate**
   - Development-pilot ordinal EFA on polychoric correlations with oblique rotation and parallel-analysis support is completed before confirmation-sample model review.
   - The factor count is `1` or `2`, and any two-factor interpretation is substantively justified in the dossier without creating a scored construct with fewer than three retained indicators.
   - Each retained item has primary loading at least `0.50`.
   - Each retained item has maximum absolute secondary loading no greater than `0.30`.
   - EFA evidence supports a coherent economic-vocational anxiety construct rather than four unrelated item-level concerns.

5. **CFA gate**
   - Confirmation-sample ordered-categorical CFA using WLSMV estimates the preregistered one-factor `economic_vocational_anxiety` model without outcome-driven model modifications being treated as confirmatory.
   - CFA fit passes all strong fit thresholds: `CFI >= 0.95`, `TLI >= 0.95`, `RMSEA <= 0.06`, and `SRMR <= 0.08`.
   - Every retained item has standardized loading at least `0.50`.
   - Residual diagnostics do not show unresolved local dependence, wording artifact, scenario contamination, or construct contamination requiring item removal.
   - CFI or TLI from `0.90` to `0.949`, or RMSEA from `0.061` to `0.080`, may be described only as cautionary evidence and does not satisfy this scored-release promotion gate unless the analysis plan is amended before outcome inspection. Fit worse than those cautionary ranges blocks construct scoring.

6. **Omega reliability gate**
   - Confirmation-sample ordinal omega total is at least `0.70`.
   - The dossier reports whether omega total reaches `0.80`; reaching `0.80` may support mature reliability language, while falling below `0.80` blocks mature headline scoring readiness claims.
   - Every retained item has corrected item-total correlation at least `0.30`, unless a retained item has explicit preregistered construct-coverage justification and reviewer approval.
   - Dropping a retained item does not materially improve omega while weakening the preregistered economic-vocational coverage of skill obsolescence, wage pressure, retraining pressure, or status loss.

7. **IRT gate**
   - The confirmation-sample unidimensional graded-response IRT model converges for the retained item set.
   - Every retained item has discrimination at least `0.65`.
   - Category threshold ordering violations equal `0`, or any apparent disordering is resolved by a preregistered sensitivity analysis accepted by the reviewer before scored approval.
   - Maximum absolute local dependence is less than `0.20`.
   - Item information is adequate for the score range described in the dossier; weak tail information must narrow claims rather than be hidden.
   - IRT evidence is used for retention, response-scale functioning, and sensitivity only. `v0.5.1` may not introduce theta scoring unless a separate release document defines and validates it.

8. **DIF gate**
   - DIF analyses are completed for all preregistered grouping variables with adequate cell sizes: age group, gender, race and ethnicity, education, Census region, employment status, occupation group, current labor-force attachment, prior AI exposure, AI-news exposure, perceived occupational AI exposure, baseline general anxiety, and device type.
   - Ordinal logistic DIF uses Benjamini-Hochberg FDR control within the four-item family, with IRT DIF sensitivity where subgroup sizes permit.
   - No retained item has unresolved statistically supported and practically meaningful DIF.
   - Practical DIF blocks scored approval or narrows claims when pseudo-`R^2` change is at least `0.02`, expected-score difference is at least `0.10` standard deviations, or rank-order impact changes interpretation.
   - Sparse groups may be reported descriptively, but sparse-cell handling may not authorize subgroup score claims unless the sensitivity analysis shows stable conclusions.

9. **Invariance gate**
   - Configural ordered-categorical CFA converges and has acceptable fit for every adequately powered comparison group whose scores the release proposes to compare.
   - Metric invariance passes with `metric_delta_cfi >= -0.010` and `metric_delta_rmsea <= 0.015`.
   - Scalar or threshold invariance passes with `scalar_delta_cfi >= -0.010` and `scalar_delta_rmsea <= 0.015`.
   - If CFA invariance is unstable, the preregistered IRT linking fallback is documented and must reach an equivalent conclusion before any comparison claim is allowed.
   - Any partial-invariance solution is explicitly documented, reviewer-approved, limited to the affected comparison scope, and cannot be used to imply unrestricted subgroup comparability.
   - Unresolved invariance blocks subgroup mean comparisons even if item retention, CFA, omega, IRT, and DIF otherwise pass.

10. **External-validity gate**
    - Convergent validity with perceived occupational AI exposure is in the preregistered Spearman range `[0.15, 0.45]`.
    - Discriminant validity against baseline general anxiety passes with `baseline_general_anxiety_spearman <= 0.70`.
    - Criterion validity for labor-market worry passes with `labor_market_worry_odds_ratio >= 1.20`.
    - Incremental validity passes with `incremental_r2_delta >= 0.01` beyond the prespecified demographic, AI exposure, employment, occupation, and baseline anxiety covariates.
    - The dossier reports domain-specific item evidence for the focal item comparators where measured, but a focal subgroup association cannot rescue an item with weak loading, failed reliability, unresolved DIF, failed invariance, or inadequate IRT evidence.

11. **Reviewer signoff**
    - The validation dossier records independent reviewer signoff for the retained item set, failed or non-retained items, scoring decision, comparison scope, blocked uses, checksum integrity, and claim limits.
    - The signoff is dated no later than the `v0.5.1` release date.
    - The reviewer confirms that no threshold, exclusion rule, item-retention rule, comparison rule, external-validity rule, checksum requirement, or claim limit was changed after outcome inspection.
    - The reviewer confirms that observed evidence, not planned evidence alone, supports every enabled scoring field in `releases/v0.5.1/manifest.json`.

12. **Checksum integrity**
    - `releases/v0.5.1/manifest.json` checksums every required input listed in this gate.
    - Item-file checksums in the manifest match the updated item files exactly.
    - Evidence-manifest checksums match the observed results, dossier, provenance, scripts or notebooks, session lock, and generated outputs.
    - The manifest contains no future-dated release date, no missing checksum for a required artifact, and no checksum that disagrees with the evidence manifest.
    - Any regenerated artifact after reviewer signoff requires renewed checksum calculation and reviewer confirmation before release.

## Required Release Changes If Passed

If all pass checks are satisfied, `v0.5.1` must make exactly these scored-release changes:

- Set `release_status` to `citable` in `releases/v0.5.1/manifest.json`.
- Preserve the frozen item wording, item versions, response anchors, scenario text, scoring keys, construct IDs, and domain metadata from `v0.5.0`.
- Change retained economic-vocational items only from `release_status: development_only` to `release_status: approved_scored`.
- Set each retained item `validation.scoring_eligible` to `true`.
- Mark non-retained items as `development_only` for continued revision or `blocked` if the validation dossier finds a release-blocking defect that cannot be cured without new fielding.
- Populate `official_scored_items` with exactly the retained economic-vocational item IDs, in the canonical order used by `releases/v0.5.0/manifest.json`.
- Set `scoring_eligibility.official_scored_item_count` to the retained item count.
- Set `scoring_eligibility.aggregate_scoring_permitted` to `true` only for `economic_vocational_anxiety`.
- Enable only the construct mean field `economic_vocational_anxiety_mean`, computed from retained approved-scored items after preregistered respondent exclusions and item missingness rules.
- State in the eligibility rule that `economic_vocational_anxiety_mean` is the only official scored aggregate introduced by `v0.5.1`.
- Keep overall ANX-Bench scoring disabled.
- Keep domain-composite scoring disabled unless it is exactly the same as `economic_vocational_anxiety_mean` and is not labeled as an overall ANX score.
- Add `validation/v0.5/economic_vocational_anxiety/observed_wave5_results.json`, `wave5_calibration_dossier.json`, `wave5_evidence_provenance.json`, and `wave5_evidence_manifest.json` to `checksums.files` with nonzero SHA-256 digests.
- Update the validation dossier from planned evidence to observed evidence, including retained items, observed EFA, CFA, omega, IRT, DIF, invariance, external-validity results, sensitivity analyses, item-retention decisions, reviewer signoff, and blocked claims.
- Update the evidence manifest so every observed output, script or notebook, restricted dataset hash, derived-data hash, session-info hash, reviewer-signoff hash, and release-input hash is nonzero and agrees with the observed-results artifact.

No other ANX-Bench domain may become aggregate-score eligible in this promotion. Overall ANX-Bench headline scoring must remain disabled unless a later release validates the required cross-domain construct set through a separate preregistered promotion gate.

## Blocked Outcomes

Any condition below blocks promotion and keeps the release non-citable for scored economic-vocational claims:

- Fewer than `3` retained economic-vocational items.
- Wave 5 observed results are missing, schema-invalid, inconsistent with the validation dossier, or contain any zero-filled checksum.
- The validation dossier is missing, schema-invalid, incomplete, still records planned rather than observed evidence, or lacks reviewer signoff.
- Evidence provenance or evidence manifest is missing, checksum-inconsistent, or unable to link raw restricted inputs, scripts, session lock, generated outputs, observed results, dossier, and release manifest.
- Updated item files are missing, checksum-inconsistent, or change respondent-facing wording, item versions, response anchors, scenario text, scoring keys, construct IDs, or domain metadata from the frozen `v0.5.0` candidate.
- `releases/v0.5.1/manifest.json` is missing, schema-invalid, future-dated, checksum-inconsistent, or disagrees with the retained item IDs recorded in the observed results.
- Development-pilot and confirmation-sample roles are overlapping or cannot be audited.
- Exclusion flow deviates from the preregistered rules in a way that affects pass-fail decisions.
- EFA fails the loading, secondary-loading, or factor-count thresholds required by `analysis/v0.5/economic_vocational/wave5_analysis_plan.json`.
- Confirmation CFA fails any required strong fit threshold or retained-item loading threshold.
- Omega total is below `0.70`.
- Any retained item has unresolved corrected item-total, missingness, category concentration, or content-coverage failure.
- IRT discrimination, threshold ordering, item information, local dependence, convergence, or response-pattern evidence leaves an unresolved retained-item defect.
- Any retained item has unresolved practically meaningful DIF.
- Invariance is unresolved for any subgroup comparison the release proposes to authorize.
- External-validity evidence fails the preregistered convergent, discriminant, criterion, or incremental-validity rule.
- Reviewer signoff is absent, postdates the release, or does not explicitly cover scoring eligibility, item retention, checksum integrity, and claim limits.
- The manifest enables any aggregate other than `economic_vocational_anxiety_mean`.
- The manifest enables overall ANX-Bench scoring, longitudinal scoring, event-study scoring, cross-domain scoring, or claims about economic-domain trends.

When blocked, maintainers may publish a non-citable addendum documenting the failure mode, but they must leave failed or unvalidated item files as `development_only` or `blocked`; leave `official_scored_items` empty or limited to no scored construct; keep `aggregate_scoring_permitted: false`; and keep `economic_vocational_anxiety_mean`, overall ANX, longitudinal scoring, event-study scoring, and economic-domain claims disabled.

## Claim Limits

Until the citable `v0.5.1` manifest passes this gate, ANX-Bench materials may say only that Wave 5 is a frozen economic-vocational calibration packet with preregistered planned analyses. They must not claim validated economic-vocational scoring, national prevalence, labor-market trend evidence, event-study evidence, longitudinal change, subgroup mean differences, cross-domain ranking, or overall ANX-Bench movement.

If `v0.5.1` passes, permitted claims are limited to the validation scope recorded in the dossier and manifest:

- A retained-item `economic_vocational_anxiety_mean` is available for the preregistered US English online adult Wave 5 calibration context.
- The score is supported by the observed EFA, CFA, omega, IRT, DIF, invariance, external-validity, sensitivity, and reviewer evidence archived for `v0.5.1`.
- Subgroup comparisons are allowed only for grouping variables whose DIF and invariance gates passed with adequate sample support.
- External-validity language must use the preregistered economic-vocational comparators and must not imply causal labor-market effects.

Even after a passed `v0.5.1` release, maintainers and downstream users must not make longitudinal, event-study, economic-domain trend, cross-domain, or overall ANX-Bench claims until a later citable manifest validates the required repeated-wave or cross-domain evidence under its own preregistered gate.
