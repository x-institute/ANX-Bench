# ANX-Bench v0.7.0 to v0.7.1 Cross-Domain Bridge Promotion Gate

This document is the release-blocking checklist for using the frozen `v0.7.0` cross-domain bridge packet as evidence for any later aggregate-score proposal. It applies only to the Wave 7 bridge study `anx_us_2026w07_cross_domain_bridge` and only to the 12 frozen item versions listed in `releases/v0.7.0/manifest.json`.

Passing this gate does not itself authorize an overall ANX score, cross-domain score, domain-combined score, event-study estimate, longitudinal trend, clinical interpretation, individual-level classification, or policy-decision use. A passed gate permits only a later separately preregistered aggregate-score proposal. That later proposal must define the scoring model, contributing constructs, weights, uncertainty reporting, missingness rules, subgroup-comparability limits, release criteria, and claim boundaries before any official aggregate score is calculated or publicized.

Failing any required check keeps all aggregate-score proposals blocked.

## Required Inputs

The reviewer must verify the following repository artifacts before any `v0.7.1` bridge-evidence release can be considered:

```bash
python3 tools/validate_content_validity_dossier.py validation/v0.7/cross_domain_bridge/content_validity_dossier.json
python3 tools/validate_release.py releases/v0.7.1/manifest.json
```

The frozen source packet is:

releases/v0.7.0/manifest.json

The required observed evidence artifacts for a later `v0.7.1` bridge-evidence release are:

The `v0.7.1` manifest must checksum the observed results, content-validity dossier, bridge evidence, evidence provenance, evidence manifest, Wave 7 preregistration, no-event registry, analysis plan, construct registry, all schema dependencies, and all 12 item files. Every checksum must be a lowercase nonzero SHA-256 digest. Any zero-filled, missing, or stale checksum blocks promotion.

All required inputs must agree on:

- `study_id: anx_us_2026w07_cross_domain_bridge`
- `wave_id: anx_us_2026w07_cross_domain_bridge`
- `benchmark_version` lineage from `v0.7.0` to `v0.7.1`
- `event_id: no_event`
- `release_status` for Wave 7 as bridge evidence only
- the exact somatic item allowlist: `sleep_disruption_ai_news`, `body_vigilance_model_release`, `background_dread_ai_progress`, and `avoidance_after_ai_capability_demo`
- the exact economic item allowlist: `skill_obsolescence_software`, `wage_pressure_customer_support`, `retraining_pressure_accounting`, and `status_loss_creative_work`
- the exact epistemic item allowlist: `deepfake_evidence_trust`, `synthetic_news_provenance`, `ai_expert_claim_conflict`, and `personalized_misinformation_targeting`
- somatic item version `v0.2.0`
- economic and epistemic item version `v0.1.0`

## Pass Checklist

The bridge may support a later aggregate-score proposal only if every item below is true.

1. **Artifact integrity**
   - The observed results, content-validity dossier, bridge evidence, evidence provenance, evidence manifest, and `v0.7.1` manifest exist and are checksum-bound.
   - The completed content-validity dossier passes `python3 tools/validate_content_validity_dossier.py validation/v0.7/cross_domain_bridge/content_validity_dossier.json` before any psychometric or bridge promotion evidence is considered.
   - The `v0.7.1` manifest keeps `official_scored_items: []` unless a separate preregistered scored-release gate has already approved the retained contributing constructs.
   - The evidence provenance identifies the restricted respondent-item file, respondent covariate file, fielding disposition file, analysis script or notebook, software session lock, generated outputs, and reviewer signoff record.
   - The no-event registry remains unchanged, with `event_id: no_event` for every respondent-item row.

2. **Sample and exclusion integrity**
   - Analytic `N` is at least `1200`; the target `N=1500` and the achieved eligible sample are both reported.
   - The bridge sample is independent of prior Wave 5 and Wave 6 calibration respondents when panel identifiers or protected linkage tokens permit audit.
   - Exclusions follow the preregistered adult US English consent, attention, cross-domain attribution, scenario comprehension, speeding, straightline plus quality failure, excessive ANX missingness, duplicate, fraud, withdrawal, and nonunderstanding rules.
   - High baseline anxiety, high AI exposure, unemployment, occupational transition, institutional trust, news exposure, device type, and open-text distress are retained as analysis variables or sensitivity flags rather than post hoc exclusions.

3. **Item and administration integrity**
   - All 12 administered item files preserve frozen wording, response anchors, scenario text, scoring keys, item versions, construct IDs, domain IDs, and quality-control metadata.
   - Domain block order and within-block item order are randomized as preregistered and audited for balance.
   - Missingness, breakoff, technical failure, and non-substantive response rates are reported for each item before and after exclusions.
   - No item has a response distribution that makes the preregistered factor, reliability, IRT, DIF, or invariance models uninterpretable.

4. **Factor-structure gate**
   - Ordinal EFA supports three interpretable correlated domains rather than a purely unidimensional item set or unrelated item fragments.
   - Each retained item has primary loading at least `0.50` on its preregistered domain.
   - Each retained item has maximum absolute secondary loading no greater than `0.30`, or the dossier treats the cross-loading as a bridge risk that blocks simple aggregation.
   - The correlated three-factor CFA fits at least as well as the preregistered thresholds in the analysis plan and is substantively interpretable.
   - A unidimensional overall ANX model is not treated as sufficient unless it is empirically superior, interpretable, and does not erase domain-specific variance.

5. **Reliability gate**
   - Ordinal omega is at least `0.70` in each domain for early bridge readiness.
   - Any mature headline-score proposal requires omega at least `0.80` in each contributing domain or a preregistered justification showing why lower reliability does not distort aggregate interpretation.
   - Corrected item-total correlations and item information support the retained domain structure.
   - A strong somatic result cannot rescue weak economic or epistemic reliability, and a strong general factor cannot rescue a failed domain.

6. **IRT linking gate**
   - The multidimensional graded-response IRT model converges without unresolved threshold disordering, severe local dependence, or unusable item information.
   - Every retained item has discrimination at least `0.65`.
   - Absolute anchor drift for the somatic source construct is no greater than `0.20` standard deviations.
   - Linking standard error is no greater than `0.15` theta units across the central 80 percent of the observed trait range.
   - IRT evidence may support linking diagnostics only. It may not introduce official theta scoring without a later scored-release specification.

7. **DIF and invariance gate**
   - DIF is screened for age group, gender, race and ethnicity, education, Census region, employment status, occupation group, prior AI exposure, AI-news exposure, baseline general anxiety, domain block order, device type, and language-administration compliance when cell sizes permit.
   - No retained item has unresolved statistically supported and practically meaningful DIF.
   - Configural invariance converges for every adequately powered comparison the later proposal would describe.
   - Metric and scalar or threshold invariance pass with `delta CFI >= -0.010` and `delta RMSEA <= 0.015`, or a preregistered IRT-linking fallback reaches an equivalent reviewer-approved conclusion.
   - Failed invariance blocks subgroup, longitudinal, cross-domain, and overall comparisons affected by that failure.

8. **Cross-domain correlation gate**
   - Each pairwise latent correlation among somatic, economic, and epistemic domains is inside the preregistered absolute band `0.30` to `0.75`.
   - Correlations below `0.30` block common-family aggregate readiness.
   - Correlations above `0.75` block discriminant-validity support for simple aggregation.
   - The dossier reports confidence intervals, sensitivity to exclusions and weights, and whether correlations are stable across preregistered demographic and AI-exposure strata.

9. **General-factor readiness gate**
   - Bifactor or second-order models converge and are compared with the correlated-domain model.
   - Overall-score readiness requires omega hierarchical at least `0.50` and explained common variance at least `0.50`.
   - Each domain-specific factor must retain interpretable residual or specific variance.
   - If the general factor is weak, overall ANX proposals are blocked.
   - If the specific factors collapse, domain-stratified interpretation and simple domain comparisons are blocked.

10. **Reviewer signoff**
    - An independent reviewer confirms that no threshold, exclusion rule, item-retention rule, comparison rule, checksum requirement, or claim limit was changed after outcome inspection.
    - The reviewer signs off on sample integrity, no-event status, item integrity, factor structure, reliability, IRT linking, DIF, invariance, cross-domain correlations, general-factor readiness, sensitivity analyses, and blocked claims.
    - The reviewer explicitly states whether the evidence supports only `bridge_supported_domain_only`, supports `bridge_supported_for_overall_readiness_review`, or remains `blocked`.
    - The signoff is dated no later than the `v0.7.1` release date.

## Required Release State If Passed

If all pass checks are satisfied, `v0.7.1` may be published only as a citable bridge-evidence release with tightly bounded language:

- It may state that Wave 7 provides observed cross-domain bridge evidence under the preregistered US English online adult design.
- It may report whether the bridge decision is `bridge_supported_domain_only` or `bridge_supported_for_overall_readiness_review`.
- It may identify which item, domain, or model diagnostics support or limit any later aggregate-score proposal.
- It must keep `aggregate_scoring_permitted: false` unless a separate scored-release gate has independently approved every contributing construct and the aggregate model.
- It must not introduce an official overall ANX score, cross-domain score, domain-combined score, longitudinal trend, event-study estimate, clinical interpretation, individual-level classification, or policy-decision ranking.
- It must state that passing the bridge is necessary but not sufficient for aggregate scoring.

## Blocked Outcomes

Any condition below blocks use of Wave 7 as support for an aggregate-score proposal:

- The observed results, bridge evidence, evidence provenance, evidence manifest, or `v0.7.1` manifest is missing, schema-invalid, checksum-inconsistent, or contains a zero-filled checksum.
- The content-validity dossier is missing, fails schema validation, fails recomputed CVI or unresolved-flag checks, lacks required facet coverage, or signs off on promotion without machine-checkable eligibility.
- The no-event registry is altered, ignored, or reinterpreted as a capability event, shock, baseline, follow-up, or trend wave.
- Analytic `N` is below `1200`.
- Exclusion flow deviates from the preregistered rules in a way that affects pass-fail decisions.
- Any administered item changes wording, response anchors, scenario text, scoring key, item version, construct ID, or domain ID.
- EFA, CFA, reliability, IRT linking, DIF, invariance, or cross-domain-correlation gates fail.
- Pairwise latent correlations fall outside the `0.30` to `0.75` band.
- Bifactor or second-order evidence is insufficient for the specific aggregate proposal being contemplated.
- Reviewer signoff is absent, post hoc, incomplete, or inconsistent with the observed artifacts.
- The proposed claim extends beyond the observed bridge evidence.

When blocked, maintainers may publish a non-citable addendum documenting the failure mode, but they must leave aggregate scoring disabled and must not imply that ANX-Bench has a validated overall, cross-domain, trend, or event-study score.

## Claim Limits

Until a later release passes this gate and then separately passes any aggregate-score gate it proposes, ANX-Bench materials may say only that `v0.7.0` is a frozen cross-domain bridge packet with planned analyses. They must not claim validated overall ANX movement, cross-domain ranking, national prevalence, temporal trend, model-release response, event-study effect, domain-combined severity, clinical status, individual risk, or policy-decision relevance.

If a later `v0.7.1` bridge-evidence release passes this gate, permitted claims remain limited to bridge evidence. The release may describe observed psychometric readiness, limitations, and whether a later aggregate-score proposal is allowed to be preregistered. It may not itself turn bridge evidence into an official score.
