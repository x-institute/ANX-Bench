# ANX-Bench v0.8.0 to v0.8.1 Full-Domain Bridge Promotion Gate

This document is the release-blocking checklist for converting the frozen `v0.8.0` full-domain bridge packet into a citable `v0.8.1` full-domain bridge-readiness evidence release. It applies only to the Wave 8 study `anx_us_2026w08_full_domain_bridge` and only to the 24 frozen item versions listed in `releases/v0.8.0/manifest.json`.

Passing this gate authorizes only citable full-domain bridge-readiness evidence. It does not authorize scored items, domain scores, cross-domain scores, an overall ANX score, event-study estimates, longitudinal trends, clinical interpretation, diagnostic classification, individual-level decisions, policy rankings, or causal capability-shock claims. A passing decision may support a later separately preregistered scoring or aggregate-readiness proposal, but it cannot itself create any official ANX-Bench score.

Failing any required check keeps Wave 8 non-citable for full-domain bridge-readiness claims and keeps all v0.8 scoring, trend, event-study, clinical, and policy claims blocked.

## Required Inputs

The promotion reviewer must verify the following commands from the repository root before `v0.8.1` can be labeled citable bridge-readiness evidence:

```bash
python3 tools/validate_full_domain_observed_results.py validation/v0.8/full_domain_bridge/observed_wave8_results.json
python3 tools/validate_content_validity_dossier.py validation/v0.8/full_domain_bridge/content_validity_dossier.json
python3 tools/validate_full_domain_bridge_evidence.py validation/v0.8/full_domain_bridge/wave8_full_domain_bridge_evidence.json

```

The observed-results ledger command is first because it machine-checks the fielded Wave 8 record before any reviewer interprets content-validity evidence or bridge-readiness evidence. It rejects template status, future dates, missing outcome-inspection timing, analytic `N < 2000`, post hoc split timing, item/version drift, and any accidental scoring authorization.

## Pass Checklist

The release may be promoted only if every item below is true.

1. **Artifact integrity**
   - `observed_wave8_results.json`, `content_validity_dossier.json`, `wave8_full_domain_bridge_evidence.json`, `wave8_evidence_provenance.json`, `wave8_evidence_manifest.json`, and `releases/v0.8.1/manifest.json` exist at the required paths.
   - The completed observed-results ledger passes `tools/validate_full_domain_observed_results.py` before content-validity or bridge-evidence validation is run.
   - The completed content-validity dossier passes `tools/validate_content_validity_dossier.py` before bridge evidence is reviewed.
   - The full-domain bridge evidence passes `tools/validate_full_domain_bridge_evidence.py`.
   - The release manifest passes `tools/validate_release.py`.
   - The evidence provenance identifies the restricted respondent-item file, respondent covariate file, fielding disposition file, analysis script or notebook, software session lock, generated outputs, disclosure-review record, and reviewer signoff record.
   - The evidence manifest links all observed outputs, raw-input hashes, derived-data hashes, script or notebook hashes, session locks, reviewer records, and release inputs with matching nonzero checksums.
   - No observed evidence file, provenance file, manifest, dossier, or item file contains a zero-filled checksum placeholder.

2. **Sample, split, and exclusion integrity**
   - Analytic `N` after preregistered exclusions is at least `2000`. If analytic `N < 2000`, the decision is `blocked_underpowered`.
   - The target eligible analytic sample, achieved eligible sample, excluded sample, weighted descriptive sample, and unweighted analytic sample are all reported.
   - The eligible analytic sample is randomly split before outcome inspection into non-overlapping EFA calibration and CFA confirmation halves, stratified by sample source, device category, and domain-block order as preregistered.
   - Primary pass-fail EFA decisions use only the EFA half, and primary pass-fail CFA, omega, IRT, DIF, invariance, latent-correlation, anchor-drift, and aggregate-readiness decisions use only the confirmation half unless the analysis plan explicitly assigns a different role before outcome inspection.
   - Exclusions follow the preregistered adult US English consent, residence, unaided-language completion, duplicate, fraud, attention, scenario-comprehension, full-domain attribution, speeding, straightline plus quality failure, excessive ANX missingness, nonunderstanding, withdrawal, and platform-error rules.
   - High general anxiety, high AI-news exposure, high prior AI use, prior AI companion use, cybersecurity or infrastructure employment, caregiving role, urbanicity, relationship status, occupational transition, mobile completion, one low item response time, within-domain straightlining, open-text confusion, and open-text distress are retained as analysis variables or sensitivity flags rather than post hoc exclusions.

3. **Item and administration integrity**
   - All 24 administered item files preserve frozen wording, scenario text, response anchors, scoring keys, item versions, construct IDs, domain IDs, quality-control metadata, and release-line lineage.
   - The seven domain blocks and within-block item orders follow the preregistered randomization and balance checks.
   - Missingness, breakoff, technical failure, non-substantive response, and item response-time distributions are reported for each item before and after exclusions.
   - No item has a response distribution that makes the preregistered EFA, CFA, omega, IRT, DIF, or invariance models uninterpretable.
   - Somatic items retain their prior source-release status only outside the Wave 8 packet. In Wave 8 they function as bridge anchors and are not re-promoted, rescored, or expanded into a new aggregate.

4. **Split-sample EFA gate**
   - Ordinal EFA on the calibration half uses polychoric correlations, an oblique rotation, and the preregistered factor-retention procedure.
   - The EFA supports an interpretable seven-domain solution rather than a unidimensional item set, unrelated item fragments, or collapsed domains that erase preregistered domain distinctions.
   - Each retained item has primary loading at least `0.40` on its intended domain factor.
   - No retained item has a cross-loading at or above `0.30` unless the cross-loading is theoretically documented, remains at least `0.20` below the primary loading, and is carried forward as a bridge-readiness limitation.
   - No retained item has unresolved Heywood cases, unordered response thresholds, or residual local dependence above `0.20` after accounting for the domain factor.
   - EFA failures block full-domain bridge-readiness claims even if later full-sample descriptive models appear acceptable.

5. **Split-sample CFA gate**
   - The confirmation half estimates a correlated seven-factor ordinal CFA using an estimator appropriate for ordered categorical indicators.
   - The correlated seven-factor model fits better than a unidimensional model and better than collapsed-domain alternatives that merge distinct theoretical domains.
   - Confirmatory adequacy requires `CFI >= 0.950`, `TLI >= 0.940`, `RMSEA <= 0.060` with upper 90 percent confidence bound no greater than `0.080`, and `SRMR <= 0.080`.
   - Every retained item has standardized intended loading at least `0.50`, except that one item in a three-item candidate domain may load between `0.40` and `0.50` only with preregistered retention rationale and reviewer approval.
   - No absolute residual correlation above `0.20` remains unresolved within or across domains.
   - Outcome-driven model modifications may be reported as exploratory diagnostics only and cannot satisfy this confirmatory gate.

6. **Omega reliability gate**
   - Ordinal omega total is at least `0.70` for every domain to support full-domain bridge interpretation.
   - Omega total is at least `0.80` for any domain that the evidence release describes as ready for mature headline-score consideration.
   - Corrected item-total correlations, item information, and domain coverage support each retained item.
   - A strong result in one domain cannot rescue a failed reliability result in another domain.
   - Omega hierarchical and explained common variance for any proposed general ANX factor are each at least `0.50` before the evidence may support a later aggregate-readiness review. Wave 8 itself still authorizes no aggregate score.

7. **Graded-response IRT gate**
   - Domain-specific or multidimensional graded-response IRT models converge for all retained items without unresolved threshold disordering.
   - Every retained item has discrimination at least `0.65`.
   - Material local dependence is absent or handled through a preregistered sensitivity analysis that does not change the release decision.
   - Item information is stable across the central 80 percent of the observed trait range relevant to each domain.
   - Linking standard error is no greater than `0.15` theta units for bridge comparisons used in the evidence release.
   - IRT evidence may support item-functioning and bridge diagnostics only. It may not introduce official theta scoring without a later scored-release specification.

8. **DIF gate**
   - DIF is screened for age group, gender, race and ethnicity, education, Census region, employment status, occupation group, prior AI exposure, AI-news exposure, device type, sample source, and language-administration compliance when cell sizes permit.
   - Material DIF is defined as Benjamini-Hochberg FDR `q < 0.05` plus at least one practical-impact criterion: pseudo-`R^2` change at least `0.02`, expected-score difference at least `0.10` standard deviations, visible threshold shift in the central trait range, or material rank-order impact.
   - No retained item has unresolved statistically supported and practically meaningful DIF.
   - Sparse groups may be reported descriptively, but sparse-cell handling cannot authorize subgroup, domain, or aggregate comparability claims.
   - Unresolved material DIF blocks subgroup comparison for the affected item or domain and may block full-domain bridge readiness if it changes domain interpretation.

9. **Invariance gate**
   - Configural invariance converges for every prespecified grouping variable with adequate cell sizes.
   - Metric invariance passes with `delta CFI >= -0.010` and `delta RMSEA <= 0.015`.
   - Scalar or threshold invariance passes with `delta CFI >= -0.010` and `delta RMSEA <= 0.015`.
   - A preregistered IRT-linking fallback may substitute only if CFA invariance is unstable and the reviewer confirms equivalent evidence for the relevant comparison.
   - Partial invariance may support descriptive bridge evidence only when noninvariant parameters are documented and do not alter domain ordering, bridge decision, or claim boundaries.
   - Failed invariance blocks subgroup, longitudinal, cross-domain, and aggregate-readiness claims affected by that failure.

10. **Latent-correlation bounds**
    - Every pairwise latent correlation among the seven domains is positive, theoretically coherent, and inside the preregistered absolute band `0.20` to `0.80`.
    - Correlations below `0.20` block claims that the pair belongs to a common ANX family without further evidence.
    - Correlations above `0.80` block simple aggregation and signal possible construct collapse, method dominance, or insufficient discriminant validity.
    - The evidence reports confidence intervals, weighted and unweighted sensitivity, exclusion sensitivity, and stability across preregistered demographic and AI-exposure strata.
    - Correlation evidence may support only bridge-readiness interpretation. It may not be converted into an official domain score, cross-domain score, or overall ANX score.

11. **Somatic-anchor drift**
    - Somatic bridge-anchor estimates are compared with the source scoring context documented for the citable somatic release line.
    - Mean absolute somatic anchor drift is no greater than `0.20` standard deviations.
    - No single somatic anchor item shows drift greater than `0.30` standard deviations unless source-anchor use in Wave 8 bridge claims is blocked.
    - Drift sensitivity analyses report the effect of weights, exclusions, domain-block order, device type, sample source, AI-news exposure, and baseline general anxiety.
    - Somatic anchor stability may support bridge-readiness evidence only. It cannot expand the somatic construct, revise its score, or authorize a longitudinal comparison.

12. **Aggregate-readiness status**
    - The bridge decision is exactly one of `blocked_underpowered`, `blocked_psychometric`, `bridge_supported_domain_only`, or `bridge_supported_for_later_aggregate_readiness_review`.
    - `bridge_supported_domain_only` requires acceptable item administration, dimensionality, omega, IRT, DIF, invariance, latent-correlation, and somatic-anchor evidence for domain-level bridge interpretation, while preserving the prohibition on scored domains.
    - `bridge_supported_for_later_aggregate_readiness_review` additionally requires evidence that a later general-factor or aggregate-score proposal could be preregistered without erasing domain-specific variance or violating latent-correlation bounds.
    - Any failed EFA, CFA, omega, IRT, DIF, invariance, latent-correlation, somatic-anchor drift, artifact-integrity, or sample-size gate yields `blocked_underpowered` or `blocked_psychometric`, as applicable.
    - The aggregate-readiness status is a review label, not a score authorization.

13. **Reviewer signoff**
    - An independent reviewer signs the release decision no later than the `v0.8.1` release date.
    - The reviewer confirms that no thresholds, exclusions, scoring rules, item-retention rules, comparison rules, checksum requirements, claim boundaries, or aggregate-readiness labels changed after outcome inspection.
    - The reviewer confirms that the content-validity dossier was reviewed before psychometric bridge evidence.
    - The reviewer confirms that every enabled claim in `releases/v0.8.1/manifest.json` is supported by observed evidence rather than planned evidence alone.
    - The signoff explicitly covers sample integrity, no-event status, item integrity, split-sample EFA and CFA, omega, graded-response IRT, DIF, invariance, latent-correlation bounds, somatic-anchor drift, aggregate-readiness status, sensitivity analyses, checksum integrity, and blocked claims.

## Required Release State If Passed

If all pass checks are satisfied, `v0.8.1` may be published only as a citable full-domain bridge-readiness evidence release with tightly bounded language:

- It may state that Wave 8 provides observed full-domain bridge-readiness evidence under the preregistered US English online adult design.
- It may report the approved bridge decision: `bridge_supported_domain_only` or `bridge_supported_for_later_aggregate_readiness_review`.
- It may describe item, domain, model, reliability, IRT, DIF, invariance, latent-correlation, and somatic-anchor diagnostics that support or limit future readiness review.
- It must keep `official_scored_items: []`.
- It must keep `scoring_eligibility.aggregate_scoring_permitted: false`.
- It must state that passing this gate is necessary but not sufficient for scored-item, domain-score, cross-domain-score, or overall-score release.
- It must not introduce scored items, domain scores, cross-domain scores, an overall ANX score, event-study estimates, longitudinal trends, clinical interpretation, diagnostic classification, individual-level decisions, policy rankings, or causal capability-shock claims.

## Blocked Outcomes

Any condition below blocks promotion:

- Any required observed input is missing, schema-invalid, checksum-inconsistent, future-dated beyond the release, or contains a zero-filled checksum.
- The content-validity dossier fails reviewer independence, I-CVI, S-CVI/Ave, facet coverage, unresolved-flag, or signoff checks.
- The no-event registry is altered, ignored, or reinterpreted as a capability event, shock, baseline, follow-up, trend wave, or event-study window.
- Analytic `N` is below `2000`.
- EFA and CFA sample roles overlap or are assigned after outcome inspection.
- Exclusion flow deviates from the preregistered rules in a way that affects any pass-fail decision.
- Any administered item changes wording, scenario text, response anchors, scoring key, item version, construct ID, domain ID, or required quality-control metadata.
- The seven-domain EFA or CFA gate fails.
- Any domain has ordinal omega below `0.70`.
- The graded-response IRT gate fails for any retained item or domain needed for bridge interpretation.
- Any retained item has unresolved practically meaningful DIF.
- Invariance is unresolved for a comparison that the evidence release proposes to describe as comparable.
- Any pairwise latent correlation falls outside the absolute `0.20` to `0.80` band.
- Mean absolute somatic-anchor drift exceeds `0.20` standard deviations, or any single somatic anchor item exceeds `0.30` standard deviations without blocking anchor use.
- The aggregate-readiness status is missing, not one of the four permitted labels, or inconsistent with the observed gate results.
- Reviewer signoff is absent, post hoc, incomplete, inconsistent with the observed artifacts, or fails to confirm that no thresholds, exclusions, scoring rules, item-retention rules, or claim boundaries changed after outcome inspection.
- The `v0.8.1` manifest enables any scored item, domain score, cross-domain score, overall ANX score, longitudinal use, event-study use, clinical use, individual-level use, or policy-decision use.

When blocked, maintainers may publish a non-citable addendum documenting the failure mode, but they must leave scoring disabled and must not imply that ANX-Bench has a validated full-domain score, domain score, cross-domain score, overall score, longitudinal index, event-study estimate, clinical scale, or policy instrument.

## Claim Limits

Until a later `v0.8.1` manifest passes this gate, ANX-Bench materials may say only that `v0.8.0` is a frozen full-domain bridge packet with planned analyses. They must not claim validated full-domain bridge readiness, validated new domain scoring, national prevalence, temporal trend, model-release response, event-study effect, subgroup comparability, clinical severity, individual risk, or policy-decision relevance.

If `v0.8.1` passes, permitted claims remain limited to citable full-domain bridge-readiness evidence. The release may describe observed readiness, blocked domains or comparisons, sensitivity analyses, and whether a later aggregate-readiness proposal is allowed to be preregistered. It may not itself convert bridge evidence into scored items, domain scores, cross-domain scores, an overall ANX score, event-study estimates, longitudinal claims, clinical claims, or policy claims.
